How to Write an Effective CV/Resume

A well-written CV (Curriculum Vitae) or resume is essential for making a strong impression on potential employers. It should highlight your qualifications, skills, and experiences that are most relevant to the position you're applying for.

Do's

  • Tailor your CV to each job application
  • Use clear, concise language
  • Quantify achievements when possible
  • Use action verbs to describe experiences
  • Keep formatting consistent
  • Proofread carefully for errors

Don'ts

  • Include irrelevant information
  • Use generic descriptions
  • Include personal information like age or marital status
  • Use unprofessional email addresses
  • Lie or exaggerate about skills or experiences
  • Submit without proofreading

CV/Resume Structure

1. Contact Information

  • Full name (slightly larger than other text)
  • Professional email address
  • Phone number
  • City and country/region
  • LinkedIn profile or professional website (optional)

2. Professional Summary or Objective

A brief statement (2-4 sentences) highlighting your key qualifications, skills, and career goals that are relevant to the position you're applying for.

Example: "Dedicated English language teacher with 5+ years of experience in secondary education. Skilled in curriculum development, interactive teaching methodologies, and student engagement. Passionate about creating supportive learning environments that foster student growth and achievement."

3. Education

List your educational qualifications in reverse chronological order (most recent first):

  • Degree/Certificate name
  • Institution name
  • Location
  • Graduation date or expected completion date
  • GPA (if notable)
  • Relevant coursework or achievements (optional)

4. Work Experience

List your professional experiences in reverse chronological order:

  • Job title
  • Company name
  • Location
  • Employment dates
  • 3-5 bullet points describing your responsibilities and achievements

Tips for writing effective bullet points:

  • Start with action verbs (e.g., "Developed," "Implemented," "Managed")
  • Quantify achievements when possible (e.g., "Increased student test scores by 15%")
  • Focus on accomplishments rather than just duties
  • Highlight skills and experiences relevant to the job you're applying for

5. Skills

List relevant skills grouped into categories:

  • Technical skills (e.g., software proficiency, programming languages)
  • Language skills
  • Soft skills (e.g., communication, leadership, teamwork)

Indicate proficiency level when appropriate.

6. Additional Sections (Optional)

Include other relevant sections as needed:

  • Certifications/Licenses
  • Publications/Presentations
  • Professional memberships
  • Volunteer experience
  • Projects
  • Awards and honors

7. References

Either include a statement like "References available upon request" or list 2-3 professional references with their:

  • Name
  • Relationship to you
  • Company/Organization
  • Contact information

Make sure to get permission before listing someone as a reference.
