How to Interpret Charts

Writing a good chart interpretation requires careful observation and clear language. Follow these steps:

  1. Introduction: Identify the type of chart and its main topic.
  2. Overview: Describe the general trend or main information shown.
  3. Details: Highlight significant data points, maximum and minimum values.
  4. Comparisons: Compare different categories or time periods.
  5. Conclusion: Summarize the key findings or draw conclusions.
